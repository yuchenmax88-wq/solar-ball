# Ball Shell Design — Solar Ball v1.0

## Overview

- Diameter: 100mm
- Wall thickness: 2mm
- Material: White semi-transparent PETG (UV-resistant)
- External surface: Matte finish (frosted) for light diffusion
- 80 sensor holes: 5mm diameter, positioned on a Fibonacci spherical lattice

## Sensor Hole Coordinates

The 80 sensor positions are generated by `firmware/scripts/generate_sensor_coords.py`
using the Fibonacci sphere algorithm. The resulting C header file at
`firmware/include/sensor_positions.h` contains the 80 unit vectors (sorted top to bottom).

### 3D Design Workflow

1. **Generate coordinates**:
   ```
   cd firmware/scripts
   python generate_sensor_coords.py
   ```
   This outputs `../include/sensor_positions.h` with all 80 coordinates.

2. **Import into CAD** (Fusion 360 / OpenSCAD / Blender):
   - Use the generated coordinates to create 5mm holes at each position
   - Each hole is a through-hole, perpendicular to the sphere surface at that point
   - The normal direction at each hole matches its unit vector

3. **Bottom open area**:
   - The bottom ~30° of the sphere (z < -0.866) has no sensor holes
   - This area has a 20mm circular opening for:
     - Internal access during assembly
     - Cable pass-through for external power (if used)
     - Mounting to the support pole

4. **Top flat area**:
   - A 30mm diameter flat area on the top for mounting the flexible solar panel
   - The flat area is 2mm recessed so the panel sits flush with the sphere surface

5. **Internal mounting**:
   - 4 mounting bosses (M3 threaded inserts) at 90° spacing on the equator plane
   - These hold the internal PCB frame in place

## Internal Frame Design (3D Printed)

The frame holds:
- ESP32 dev board
- 5 MUX chips (CD74HC4067 on small perf-boards or single custom PCB)
- 2 ADS1115 modules
- SIM7600G 4G module
- 18650 battery
- TP4056 and MT3608 modules

The frame is a cylindrical cage structure that centers inside the ball, with
the battery at the bottom (for low center of gravity) and the ESP32 near the
top (for better WiFi/cellular signal coupling).

## Assembly Order

1. Print ball shell halves (or full sphere in soluble support)
2. Print internal frame
3. Solder all 80 ALS-PT19 sensors with 10cm leads
4. Insert sensors into holes from inside, secure with dab of hot glue
5. Mount all PCBs and modules to internal frame
6. Connect sensor leads to MUX boards
7. Connect 4G antenna
8. Install battery and power modules
9. Route cables, apply silicone sealant around all holes
10. Close ball shell (screw or snap-fit)
11. Mount solar panel on top, connect to TP4056
12. Apply additional silicone sealant around the equator joint
13. Mount ball on support pole
